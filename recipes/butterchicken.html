BUTTER CHICKEN

DESCRIPTION :

Butter chicken, also known as "Murgh Makhani" in Hindi, is a beloved and iconic dish in Indian cuisine that embodies a harmonious blend of flavors and textures. This creamy and indulgent dish features tender pieces of chicken, typically marinated in a mixture of yogurt and aromatic spices such as garam masala, ginger, garlic, and chili powder. After marinating, the chicken is traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven) to achieve a smoky, charred flavor. However, it can also be prepared on a stovetop or in an oven.

The pièce de résistance of butter chicken is its rich tomato-based gravy. Tomatoes are simmered to perfection with additional spices, cream, and a generous amount of butter, which imparts a luxurious and velvety texture to the sauce. This combination of spicy marinated chicken and the luscious, slightly sweet, and tangy tomato gravy creates a dish that's incredibly flavorful and comforting. Butter chicken is typically garnished with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and served with naan bread or fragrant basmati rice, making it a mouthwatering delight that has captured the hearts and palates of people around the world.

INGREDIENTS :

  • Chicken
  • Yougurt
  • Spices
  • ginger garlic paste
  • Lemon juice
  • Butter
  • Cream
  • Sugar
  • Tomatoes

PREPARATIONS STEPS :

  1. Chicken is marinated for several hours in a mixture of lemon juice, dahi (yogurt), Kashmiri red chili, salt, garam masala, ginger paste, and garlic paste
  2. Mix together all the ingredients under chicken marinade and marinate for at least two hours or overnight for best results
  3. When you are ready to make the biryani, soak saffron strands in hot milk and rub them slightly with the back of a spoon. Set this aside.
  4. The marinated chicken is cooked in a tandoor (traditional clay oven), but may be grilled, oven-roasted, or pan-fried. It is served in a mild curry sauce that includes butter.
  5. The sauce is a tomato, garlic, and ginger-based sauce that is simmered until smooth and much of the water has evaporated
  6. There are many variations on the composition and spicing of the sauce, which is sieved so that it is velvety smooth. Spices may include cardamom, cumin, cloves, cinnamon, coriander, pepper, garam masala, and fenugreek
  7. Cashew paste may be used as a thickener and it is finally garnished with coriander
